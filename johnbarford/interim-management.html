Why Use An Interim Manager?

An interim director or manager enables you to harness the skills of a highly experienced person to fulfil the needs of your organisation at a particular moment in its life.

The Problems I Solve

An interim manager or director offers expertise at a time of change. This could mean new business development, restructuring or giving an organisation some breathing space during the recruitment of a permanent executive, while moving the organisation forward through the delivery of key objectives and developing teams.

Key Skills

  • Strategy

    Identify and develop strategic objectives and implement plans to meet these over the short, medium and long-term through the effective deployment of resources.

  • Business Planning

    Build, model and implement business plans, develop opportunities and identify cost savings. Drive sales and profitability.

  • People

    Manage, motivate and develop teams across a broad range of disciplines to ensure a lasting impact. Action full cycle recruitment and induction to the permanent role.

  • Marketing

    Manage the marketing function to understand and reach target customers/audience with the brand proposition and drive revenue. Increase brand equity.

  • Retail

    Make strategic decisions on market positioning and brand, product range, merchandising, operations and customer service.

  • Ecommerce

    Drive strategic plans to develop online trading through increasing traffic, conversion, average order value and repeat purchase.

  • Catering

    Align the offer to the target market across menu, environment and value for money. Establish KPIs, performance reporting and contract governance.

  • Publishing

    Address the commercial imperative of a publishing programme through working with editorial, production, sales and distribution teams.

  • Visitor Experience

    Manage the strategic planning and delivery of a consistent quality visitor experience that drives revenue and repeat visits.
